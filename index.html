Intro

As a retail manager, this was always my favorite hat to wear, which is why I'm looking to take a step away from management for awhile to focus on my passion for analytics.

I am excited to share my work with you. Here you will find analytical projects related to my professional and personal interests, ranging from business dashboards to sports, music, and climate-related vizzes. I am constantly learning new skills and looking forward to bringing you more projects soon! Please check out my work here.

Interactive Résumé | Tableau

What better way is there to get to know me than through interacting with my experiences? I saw a LinkedIn post one time showing some of the ways people have created interactive résumés in Tableau and I was inspired to create my own.

In this interactive résumé, you get to see my professional background in a unique way. My work and educational history are presented in a rounded Gantt chart in the center, with my hard and soft skills represented in the horizontal bubble chart below by half-circles (soft skills) and triangles (hard skills). Hover over, or click each of the elements to explore!

Retail Dashboard | Tableau

Spending the last 17 years in retail, I know there is a wide gap when it comes to data literacy and interpretation, so I wanted to create a retail dashboard that is simple, yet delivers a ton of value to field leaders. To promote data literacy and help users understand how to operate the reports, each dashboard has an instruction overlay providing basic information about how to navigate the report. The overlay can be closed or opened by selecting the "X" in the top left corner. The workbook is comprised of 3 dashboards:

Executive Dashboard - This dashboard provides a high-level overview of the business. It was built with retail managers in mind so that they can identify KPI trends at a global level.

Field Dashboard - As an ex-Store Manager, this dashboard has the information that I would expect to see as a field leader. This dashboard allows managers to dive deeper into the business by segment, category, sub-category, and customer-level data.

KPI Detail - Not everyone is a chart and graph person... This dashboard provides users the flexibility to see their business in their own way, and drill-down KPIs how they would like based on the various dimensions.

Bellabeat Case Study | SQL


This case study is the capstone project in the Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate on Coursera. I was provided a dataset from Kaggle on which to analyze trends in fitness tracker usage to answer key business questions and make high-level recommendations for a wellness brand's marketing strategy (Bellabeat).

In this project I take on the role of a data analyst working on the marketing analytics team at Bellabeat, a high-tech manufacturer of health-focused products for women. Bellabeat is a successful small company, but they aspire to become a larger player in the global smart device market. I have been tasked with focusing on one of Bellabeat's core products and analyzing smart device data to gain insight into how consumers are already using their devices. The Insights I discover will then help guide the marketing strategy for the company. I have created a presentation as a Tableau Story to present my high-level recommendations.

See this project done in R Studio

PDX Weather Analysis | R


I'm fascinated by climate change and weather related events. To develop my skills with the program language R, I pulled real climate data for the PDX weather station dating back to 1938 from NOAA.gov, to help me visualize what weather in Portland has looked like over the last 84 years.

Prior to moving to Portland in 2011, I lived in Bend, OR. Growing up, all I ever heard about was how wet and rainy Portland was. People made it sound like Portland never saw the sun. In my exploration of the data, I found some interesting facts relating to precipitation, snow, and temperature change in Portland. All this Portland data made me curious to compare it to my home town of Bend, OR.

Spoiler alert! Although the average temperature in Portland has been rising over the past 80 years, temperatures in Bend have actually declined!

Go ahead, check it out!

LeBron James Stats | Excel

For this project, I pulled dozens of tables from the nba.com stats pages and used powerquery to merge all of the files into a single Excel doc. From there, I used formulas to add several columns to the data to include location data for each game, as well as whether the game was home or away. I then used Tableau to analyze and visualize his Win/Loss and Points statistics.
